2D Design & Laser Cutting

Making flat files that can become physical objects.

Assignment

This week focused on 2D design workflows: designing a press-fit box, getting more comfortable in Fusion 360, and preparing files for laser cutting.

Make a Box

The first assignment was to design and fabricate a box. The important constraints were kerf, material thickness, and making sure the finger joints fit tightly enough to hold the box together.

Add measurements, screenshots from the design file, and photos of the assembled box here.

Fusion 360 Tutorial

I used the Fusion workflow to practice sketch constraints, dimensions, extrudes, and export steps. The goal was to make the model parametric enough that changes in material thickness or slot size could be adjusted cleanly.

Fusion Modeling

For the modeling assignment, I focused on translating a design idea into a fabrication-ready file. The next version of this page should include the original sketch, CAD screenshots, exported files, and notes on what changed after cutting.
